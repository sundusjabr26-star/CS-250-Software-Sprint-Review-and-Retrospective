**Reflection on Agile Development and Scrum Practices
Interpreting User Needs and User Stories**

Interpreting user needs begins with active engagement with stakeholders through discussions, interviews, and feedback sessions. In this course, user stories played a critical role in translating these needs into actionable development tasks. By structuring requirements in the format “As a user, I want… so that…”, user stories ensured clarity, focus, and alignment with user expectations. They helped break down complex requirements into manageable increments, making it easier to design, develop, and test features iteratively.

**Approach to Developing Programs and Agile Practices**

My approach to developing programs has evolved to emphasize iterative development, continuous feedback, and adaptability. Through this course, I have learned the importance of Agile processes such as Sprint Planning, Daily Scrums, Sprint Reviews, and Retrospectives. These practices promote transparency, improve collaboration, and ensure consistent progress. In future development work, I plan to incorporate backlog refinement, continuous testing, and incremental delivery to enhance both efficiency and product quality.

**Being an Effective Team Member in Software Development**

Being a strong team member in software development means actively collaborating, communicating clearly, and taking shared ownership of project outcomes. It involves being adaptable to change, supporting team members, and contributing to problem-solving efforts. This course demonstrated that successful Agile teams rely on trust, accountability, and continuous improvement. A good team member not only completes assigned tasks but also contributes to the overall success of the team by engaging in discussions, offering feedback, and helping remove obstacles.

**Portfolio Artifact**

This repository includes my Sprint Review and Retrospective, which reflects my understanding of Scrum roles, Agile practices, and the software development life cycle. It demonstrates how Agile principles were applied to deliver value, adapt to change, and improve team collaboration throughout the SNHU Travel project.
